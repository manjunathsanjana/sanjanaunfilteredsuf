There is a specific kind of internal compass that activates when you belong to more than one place. For me, my heart has always existed in a beautiful, shifting triangle between Mandya, Bengaluru, and Mysuru. If you mention any of these names to me, my soul instantly perks up. But if we are being completely honest, Mysuru holds a frequency that none of the others can ever touch.

Because I was born in Mandya, that city represents pure, unfiltered nostalgia. It is the place of childhood vacations, the twice-a-year escape where my heart genuinely dances the moment I arrive. But we all know the bittersweet tax of a vacation: that heavy, grieving feeling that takes over your chest when it’s finally time to pack your bags and head back. Mandya is love, but it has always been a fleeting kind of love packaged into short holidays.

With Mysuru, the relationship is entirely different. The moment I cross into its borders, a profound sense of *nemmadi*—that deep, unadulterated peace—settles into my bones. It is an instant chemical reaction. The second I land in Mysuru, my heart just knows, and it starts jumping with an excitement that has only grown louder and more vibrant over time. Walking down the streets here isn’t just transit; it’s an act of coming home. Every corner feels familiar, and I feel a quiet, certain sense of belonging.

Because of this deep connection, I’ve noticed a fiercely protective streak growing inside me. On weekends, when I see the massive influx of people creating traffic and rushing into the city, it genuinely pains me. It feels pathetic to watch because it feels like people are ruining my city, bringing a chaotic, frantic energy into a place that thrives on its quiet grace.

Mysuru isn’t just a geographic location where I happen to spend time; it is the architect of my soul. It has given me love, life, and everything that I am today. When a city gives you that much, you live with a constant, burning wish to give back to it in a good way. I hope with everything in me that I get that chance soon, because I will remain always and forever grateful to Mysuru 🥹❤️
