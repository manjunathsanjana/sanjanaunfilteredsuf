The silence at 14,000 feet is different. It’s not just the absence of sound; it’s a presence—a cold, thin, and heavy kind of quiet that makes every breath feel like a victory.

Standing there, waiting for the first light to break over the ridge, my fingers were numb, and my lungs were protesting every step. I kept asking myself: *Was it worth it?*

Then, the first sliver of gold touched the peaks. The world turned from a deep indigo to a fiery orange in a matter of seconds. In that moment, the exhaustion, the cold, and the struggle vanished.

Travel isn’t just about the destination. It’s about the person you become when you’re pushed to your limits. This trek changed me.
